01 / Rationale

Why Curating Your Information Intake Is a Political Act

Every media diet is an architecture of attention. Most people inherit theirs from algorithms, geography, and inertia. The feeds they scroll, the podcasts that autoplay, the newsletters that accumulate unchecked: these were not chosen so much as they accrued, shaped by recommendation engines whose incentives are engagement, not understanding.

This project rejects that passive accumulation. It begins from the premise that how you consume information is as consequential as what you consume. A deliberately constructed media ecosystem is not a luxury of the overinformed; it is a prerequisite for anyone who wants to think clearly about a complex world. It is an act of intellectual self-governance.

The architecture presented here was built to serve a specific set of commitments: verification over virality, depth over speed, pluralism over comfort, and global coverage that does not collapse the world into a handful of capital cities. It spans wire services that prioritize factual baselines over narrative, investigative nonprofits that hold power accountable without partisan capture, specialist outlets that go deeper than any generalist can, and primary research sources that let the reader verify claims against evidence rather than trust secondhand summaries.

This is not a list of the best media outlets. It is a portrait of what information literacy looks like when it is treated as a practice rather than a credential: conscious, structured, multilingual, and always subject to revision.

02 / Reader Profile

The Information Needs This Ecosystem Serves

This ecosystem was designed for a specific reader: a multilingual professional working at the intersection of humanitarian mapping, geospatial analysis, and open data, based in Montreal. The reader operates in English and French daily, reads Traditional Chinese for depth, studies Japanese at an intermediate level, and uses Spanish occasionally for purposeful practice. The following dimensions describe the priorities that shaped every scoring decision in this project.

Trust Architecture

Trustworthiness is the first filter. The reader prioritizes outlets with published editorial standards, transparent correction cultures, and institutional safeguards against conflicts of interest. Wire services and public broadcasters form the factual baseline. Everything else is measured against that standard.

Geographic Priorities

Canada and Quebec are daily ground. Taiwan and the Traditional Chinese mediascape are identity-level priorities. Switzerland, the United Kingdom, and France anchor the European layer. Japan, Southeast Asia, Central Asia, Russia, North and West Africa, Barbados and the Caribbean, Brazil and Latin America, and the Indigenous and Arctic world complete a global picture that refuses to leave out the periphery.

Disciplinary Stack

The reader's intellectual terrain spans AI and technology governance, humanitarian affairs and UN systems, labour rights and SDG 8, climate science and policy, philosophy and symbolic systems, digital humanities and cartography, contemporary art and curatorial practice, and architecture and spatial politics.

Portfolio Balance

The target mix is 40% institutional anchors for factual baselines, 40% independent, specialist, and nonprofit outlets for depth and correction, and 20% research and official sources for primary verification. This structure ensures that no single type of outlet dominates the information environment.

Multilingual Practice

English and French are the daily working languages. Traditional Chinese reading is maintained for Taiwan focused depth. Japanese practice uses learner-friendly news with furigana. Spanish is occasional but purposeful, using accessible international services. Arabic and Russian are accessed through translation and multilingual outlets.

Anti-Sensationalism Commitment

The reader has zero tolerance for sensationalism, engagement-optimized framing, and verification-light content. The ecosystem is designed to minimize exposure to outlets that prioritize clicks over accuracy and to maximize exposure to outlets that prioritize evidence, method, and nuance.

03 / Scoring Methodology

How Every Source Was Evaluated

Each of the 355 sources in this ecosystem was scored out of 100 using a weighted evaluation system tailored to the reader's intake profile rather than a generic media bias framework. The system distributes points across eight criteria, then applies a portfolio modifier to reward ecosystem diversity and penalize redundancy. This ensures that the final ranking reflects not just individual quality but how well each source strengthens the overall information architecture.

Portfolio Modifier: ±15 Points

After computing the base score, a portfolio adjustment of up to 15 points is applied to evaluate how the source strengthens the overall ecosystem. Points are added when a source fills an underserved geography, addresses a disciplinary gap, or increases ownership and institutional diversity through nonprofit, independent, or specialist models. Points are subtracted when a source is largely redundant with stronger outlets already in the portfolio or when it adds prestige without adding genuine perspective. This modifier ensures the ecosystem does not over-converge on large incumbents.

Research Pipeline: From Intake Form to Final Ranking

The process began with a detailed information intake self-assessment covering languages (with proficiency levels), geographic priorities, disciplinary interests, trust thresholds, preferred outlet types, and explicit anti-preferences. This structured specification was then submitted to three distinct AI research platforms in sequence.

Perplexity AI Deep Research produced a citation-grounded report of recommended outlets organized by region and topic, emphasizing outlets with published editorial standards. ChatGPT Extended Thinking Deep Research independently generated a parallel set of recommendations, catching outlets the first pass missed, particularly niche academic journals and non-English specialist sources. Finally, Claude Opus 4.6 Extended consolidated all outputs, deduplicated 355 unique sources, reconciled conflicting metadata, and computed the profile-specific fit score described above.
